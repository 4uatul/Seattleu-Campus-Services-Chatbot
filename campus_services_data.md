### Transportation & Parking Services Overview

**Breadcrumb:** Campus Services > Transportation and Parking Services  
**URL:** https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/TransportationandParkingServices.aspx  
**Contact:** Location: Pavilion 010 | Phone: 206-296-5992 | Email: ParkingServices@seattleu.edu  
**Tags:** parking, transportation, TPS, overview, permits

At Seattle University, Transportation & Parking Services (TPS) helps students, faculty, staff, and guests travel to and from campus. ### Print File Guidance

**Breadcrumb:** Campus Services > Reprographic > Print File Guidance  
**URL:** https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/TipsandTricks.aspx  
**Tags:** printing, bleeds, color, CMYK, RGB, DPI, file format

BLEEDS & MARGINS: A bleed is image/color/artwork extending beyond trim line to ensure no white border after cutting. Most printers cannot print to edge—leaves ~1/4 inch white margin. With bleed, oversized paper is used and trimmed. Leave minimum 1/4 inch margin between text and trim line. COLOR SPACES: RGB (Red, Green, Blue) used by screens (3 lights combine to white). CMYK (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Black) used for printing (4 inks combine to black). PDFs designed to translate RGB to CMYK. PNG uses RGB—not recommended for print. DPI/RESOLUTION: Screens need only 72 DPI; print needs 300 DPI minimum. For large format banners aim for 600 DPI. FILE FORMAT: PDF best. Also accepts DOC, DOCX, EPS, JPG, PSD, TIF. Not recommended: BMP, GIF, PNG, PPT, TXT. BRAND GUIDELINES: Marketing Communications (MarCom) has resources—logos, typefaces, templates, brand guidelines. MarCom manages Canva access.

Each piece marked Non-Profit Organization with correct permit number. BUSINESS REPLY MAIL: Template from MarCom or Reprographics. THIRD-PARTY: Obtain approval 10 business days before mail date. Submit ProcureSU requisition, get PO, fill out Bulk Mail Permit Preapproval Form. Postage charged plus $0.004/item.

**Q: What is a bleed in printing?**

A: A bleed is image/color/design that extends 0.125 inch (3mm) beyond the document's final trim edge, ensuring no white border appears after the page is cut.

*Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/TipsandTricks.aspx*

**Q: What's the difference between RGB and CMYK?**

A: RGB (Red, Green, Blue) is used by screens, combining 3 light hues. CMYK (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Black) is used for printing, combining 4 inks. PDFs translate RGB to CMYK for accurate print.

*Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/TipsandTricks.aspx*
