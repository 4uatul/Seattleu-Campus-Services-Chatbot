# Transportation & Parking FAQ

**Section:** Transportation & Parking
**Document type:** FAQ pairs

## Questions and Answers

### Q: Do I need a parking permit at Seattle University?

A: Yes, a valid permit is always required when parking a vehicle on campus, even on weekends, holidays, and breaks. All parking permits are virtual and connected to the vehicle's license plate.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/TransportationandParkingServices.aspx

Tags: parking, permit, required

### Q: How much does daily parking cost at Seattle University?

A: The SU Affiliate daily rate (Pay-By-The-Day) is $13.00 from 6:00 AM to 11:59 PM. The Community/Non-Affiliate daily rate is $20.00. Rates effective July 1, 2025.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/ParkingServices.aspx

Tags: parking, rates, daily

### Q: How much is a quarterly student parking permit?

A: Student quarterly permits: Day Commuter $385, On-Campus Resident $385, Evening Commuter $225, 24/7 Commuter $465, Motorcycle $110, Carpool $190.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/StudentParking.aspx

### Q: How much is faculty/staff parking per month?

A: Monthly recurring employee permits: Standard Employee Parking $155.00, Carpool $70.00, Motorcycle $37.00. Paid via payroll deduction and valid until cancelled.

### Q: How do I pay a parking citation at Seattle University?

A: Parking citations are managed by Impark. Visit www.impark.com and click 'Pay a Notice'. Seattle University's TPS no longer processes payments directly.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Citations.aspx

Tags: citation, payment, Impark

---

### Q: How do I appeal a parking citation?

A: Visit payments.impark.com and click 'Open Messenger' (red button, bottom-right) to file an appeal. Appeals only approved for clear enforcement errors such as license plate misread.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Citations.aspx

Tags: citation, appeal, Impark

---

### Q: How much is a parking citation?

A: A citation is $75.00 when issued. Penalty totals $93.00 if unpaid seven days after issuance. Towing may occur upon third unpaid citation, ADA violations, or unauthorized space use.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/TransportationandParkingServices.aspx

Tags: citation, fees

---

### Q: What happens if I have unpaid parking citations?

A: A Parking Hold may be placed on your SU account, preventing you from obtaining your diploma or transcripts. A Parking Hold does NOT prevent class registration. Balances may be referred to a collection agency.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Frequently-Asked-Questions.aspx

Tags: citation, hold, FAQ

---

### Q: Are parking permits refundable?

A: No, parking permits are non-refundable.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/ParkingRegulations.aspx

### Q: Can first-year resident students bring a car to campus?

A: No, first-year undergraduate resident students are not permitted to bring cars during their first year (fall-spring). Waiver petitions allowed for off-campus employment (min 20 hrs), medical, family, or university obligation reasons. Email parkingservices@seattleu.edu.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/ParkingRegulations.aspx

Tags: first-year, resident, waiver

---

### Q: Where are electric vehicle chargers located on campus?

A: Seattle University has 10 EV chargers: 8 at the north end of Pigott Visitor Lot (P1) and 2 in the 13th & East Cherry Lot (P6). Maximum charging time is 4 hours. Valid SU parking permit required while charging.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Electric-Vehicle-Charging.aspx

Tags: EV, electric vehicle, charging

---

### Q: How do I get guest parking for a campus visitor?

A: Submit a Virtual Guest Parking Request at least 3 business days in advance. After 1-3 business days you receive an EVITE link to forward to your guest. Rates: 0-2 hrs $6, 2-4 hrs $10, full day $15, evenings (after 5pm) $8, Sundays $8.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/DepartmentalGuestParking.aspx

Tags: guest, visitor, virtual

---

### Q: How can I rent a van from Seattle University?

A: TPS has 4 twelve-passenger vans and 1 ten-passenger wheelchair-accessible van. All drivers must complete the University Authorized Driver process. Costs: $35 (0-3 hrs), $70/day (over 3 hrs). $0.55/mile after first 100 miles. Contact vanrental@seattleu.edu.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/VehicleRentals.aspx

Tags: van, rental, vehicle

---

### Q: How do I get a subsidized ORCA transit card?

A: Students can purchase 50% subsidized ORCA products through the University at the beginning of each academic term. ORCA LIFT (reduced fare) is available for qualifying students via King County Public Health. Benefits-eligible employees can enroll in Employee ORCA.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/ORCATransitPass.aspx

Tags: ORCA, transit, subsidy

### Q: What's the speed limit on campus?

A: No vehicle or bicycle may be operated on campus roadways, parking lots, or malls at a speed more than 5 miles per hour.

### Q: Can I park overnight in my vehicle on campus?

A: No, overnight parking of occupied vehicles (motor homes, campers, trucks, cars) is not permitted. TPS may authorize short-term occupancy parking for special needs.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/ParkingRegulations.aspx

### Q: What do I need to know about Broadway Garage access?

A: Starting April 30, entry requires a valid Seattle U Campus ID Card for both vehicle and pedestrian entrances. A valid parking permit is still required. Garage monitored 24/7. Access issues: Public Safety 206-296-5990.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Broadway-Garage-Upgrades.aspx
