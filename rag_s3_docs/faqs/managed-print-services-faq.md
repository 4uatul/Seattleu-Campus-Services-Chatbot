# Managed Print Services FAQ

**Section:** Managed Print Services
**Document type:** FAQ pairs

## Questions and Answers

### Q: What is Managed Print Services (MPS)?

A: MPS is a unified campus-wide system delivering self-service printing, copying, and scanning. It manages all print-capable devices on campus except School of Law. Print to su-print-queue and release at any MPS device by tapping/swiping your card.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/ManagedPrintServices.aspx

Tags: MPS, overview

---

### Q: How do I print from a personal computer at Seattle University?

A: Download PaperCut. Mac and PC instructions available in the Print from Personal Devices support article. Then print to su-print-queue.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/ManagedPrintServices.aspx

Tags: personal, PaperCut

### Q: How much does printing cost at Seattle University?

A: First Hill: B&W $0.08 single-sided/$0.16 double-sided, Color $0.18/$0.36. SLU/Cornish: B&W $0.01/$0.02, Color $0.25/$0.50. Letter/Legal size; Tabloid (11x17) costs double.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Printing-Fees.aspx

Tags: fees, costs

---

### Q: How much print allocation do students get?

A: First Hill: $10 per quarter. SLU/Cornish: $30 per semester for non-Design students, $50 per semester for Design students. Balance rolls over quarter to quarter but resets each fall.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Student-Information.aspx

Tags: student, allocation

### Q: How do I move or add an MPS printer?

A: Only MPS can move devices. Submit form via Move/Add/Remove MPS Printer at IT Service Desk (Jira). Required for adding, removing, or moving printers—even within same room. Minimum 5 business days to process.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/MPS-Device-Move-Add-Change.aspx

Tags: MPS, move, device

---

### Q: Why is duplex (double-sided) the default for printing?

A: In Fall 2019, Seattle University set duplex as default for all printing to support a more eco-friendly environment by reducing paper use.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/MPS-FAQs.aspx

Tags: duplex, sustainability

---

### Q: What is the SU Print Queue?

A: The su-print-queue is the single shared print queue. Click 'print' on a university computer and send to su-print-queue. Then tap or swipe your card at ANY MPS machine on campus to release the job.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/ManagedPrintServices.aspx

Tags: print queue, release

---

### Q: How do I find a printer's asset number?

A: First Hill: 5-digit code with 2 letters + 3 numbers (usually starts with GS, sometimes HD or KC). Look for label on front of device. SLU/Cornish: 7-digit code starting with KC + 5 numbers.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Finding-the-Asset-Number-of-a-Printer.aspx

Tags: asset, identification

---

### Q: Can I buy my own printer for my department?

A: Purchase/reimbursement of printers and toner restricted on both campuses. Needs not covered by MPS may be eligible for an exception. Fill out MPS Program Exception Request form. Without approval, university funds cannot be used.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Program-Exceptions.aspx

Tags: exception, department

---

### Q: What software issues does MPS NOT support?

A: MPS does NOT support printing within specialized design software (Adobe InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop, QuarkXPress), color management/ICC profiles, imposition/bleed/crop marks, or third-party app print dialog settings. Test by exporting to PDF first.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Print-Support-Scope.aspx

Tags: support, scope
