# Academic Safety FAQ

**Section:** Academic Safety
**Document type:** FAQ pairs

## Questions and Answers

### Q: What do I do in a campus emergency?

A: Call Public Safety (DPS) at 206-296-5911 for incidents and emergencies. For non-emergency after-hours: 206-296-5990.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Academic-Safety-Page.aspx

Tags: emergency, DPS

### Q: How do I report a safety incident?

A: Submit an Incident Report through the Academic Safety page. For chemical/safety data, use CampusOptics or the SDS Database. Resources include Environmental Health & Safety (EHS) and Public Safety (DPS).

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Academic-Safety-Page.aspx

Tags: incident, report

---

### Q: How much funding can my department request for safety equipment?

A: Each academic department can request up to $350 per fiscal year (July 1-June 30) for reusable safety items. Multiple requests allowed; priority to departments not previously funded. Submit Safety Equipment Funding Request form with item list and explanation.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/SafetyEquipmentFunding.aspx

Tags: funding, equipment

---

### Q: What safety trainings are available?

A: Online training through EngageSU. Live in-person/virtual: Biosafety BSL1, BSL2, Bloodborne Pathogens, Compressed Gases, Cryogens, Lab Safety Orientation, Risk Assessment. Hands-on: Fire Extinguisher, PPE Donning/Doffing, Sample Transport, Train the Trainer, Biosafety Cabinet. Custom training: contact Academic Safety Officer.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/TrainingOpportunities.aspx

Tags: training, biosafety

---

### Q: What safety committees exist at Seattle University?

A: University Safety Committee (evaluates Accident Prevention Program). Institutional Biosafety Committee (IBC) (oversees recombinant nucleic acid research). Building Safety Committees at Sinegal and Bannan. Topic-specific: Lab Safety Committee, Shop Safety Committee.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/SafetyCommittees.aspx

Tags: committees, biosafety

---

### Q: Where can I find Safety Data Sheets (SDS)?

A: Safety Data Sheets are accessible through CampusOptics and the SDS Database, linked from the Academic Safety page.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Documents.aspx
