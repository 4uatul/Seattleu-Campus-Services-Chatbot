# Supercopy FAQ

**Section:** Supercopy
**Document type:** FAQ pairs

## Questions and Answers

### Q: How do I get a Campus ID Card?

A: All first-time Campus Cards are free. New freshmen and transfer students submit a photo via the Transact eAccounts Portal. Visit Supercopy (Pavilion 010) to have your card printed. Bring SU ID number and a state-issued ID.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/CampusIDCards.aspx

Tags: ID, card, new

---

### Q: How much does it cost to replace a lost Campus Card?

A: $20.00 plus tax for a replacement. Pay and pick up at Supercopy during operating hours. Temporary loaner cards are free for up to 7 days and must be returned.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/CampusIDCards.aspx

Tags: ID, replacement, lost

---

### Q: What do I do if my Campus Card is lost or stolen?

A: Log in to eAccounts (Transact eAccounts Portal) to deactivate your card immediately. Then notify Public Safety (DPS). After-hours: 206-296-5990.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/CampusIDCards.aspx

Tags: lost, stolen, deactivate

---

### Q: What does a Campus Card do?

A: Used for building access, library card, printer agent (24-hour first activation), gym pass, and meal plan transactions at Redhawk Dining.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/CampusIDCards.aspx

Tags: card, function, access

---

### Q: How do I get door access to a campus building?

A: Email Public Safety (DPS) at keys2@seattleu.edu or use the Public Safety Portal. After-hours: 206-296-5990. Have your name, SU ID, building, room number, and reason ready.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/CampusIDCards.aspx

Tags: building, access, DPS

---

### Q: What services does Supercopy provide?

A: Campus ID Cards, copying/printing, poster printing, binding/laminating, business cards, supplementary ID badges, faxing, notary services, and privilege cards.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/SuperCopy.aspx

Tags: Supercopy, services

---

### Q: How do I book notary services?

A: Schedule at least 2 hours in advance via Supercopy Notary Bookings. Hours: Mon-Thur 8:30-11:30 AM and 1:30-3:30 PM; Friday 8:30-11:30 AM. No weekend appointments. For department billing forward confirmation to repro-notaries@seattleu.edu with 17-digit accounting code.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Notary-Services.aspx

Tags: notary, appointment

---

### Q: What is a Privilege Card?

A: Privilege Cards enable campus affiliates (vendors, guests, Non-Student Sublessees) to access University privileges like door access, printing, copying, payment systems. Available through ProcureSU for an additional fee.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/Privilege-Cards.aspx

Tags: Privilege, Copy Card

---

### Q: What is a Supplementary ID Badge?

A: Supplementary ID Badges are visual identification credentials. They do NOT provide building access, printing/library privileges, or payment access. Available via ProcureSU. Designs: Generic, HRL, Facilities, ITS, HES.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/SupplementaryIDBadges.aspx

Tags: ID badge, supplementary

---

### Q: How do I get business cards as a student?

A: Law School students: Fill out LS Student Business Card Form or visit Supercopy. Business School students: Visit Albers Placement Center for form (signed by Albers staff), drop at Supercopy. Other schools: Visit Career Services for form (signed by Career Services), drop at Supercopy.

Source: https://redhawks.sharepoint.com/sites/Intranet-CampusServices/SitePages/BusinessCards.aspx

Tags: business cards, student
